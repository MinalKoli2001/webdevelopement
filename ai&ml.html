“Artificial Intelligence is a scientific discipline embracing several Data Science fields ranging from narrow AI to strong AI, including machine learning, deep learning, big data and data mining.”

-Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence are two closely related but distinct fields within the broader field of computer science. Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a discipline that focuses on creating intelligent machines that can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as visual perception, speech recognition, decision-making, and natural language processing. It involves the development of algorithms and systems that can reason, learn, and make decisions based on input data.

- On the other hand, Machine Learning (ML) is a subfield of AI that involves teaching machines to learn from data without being explicitly programmed. ML algorithms can identify patterns and trends in data and use them to make predictions and decisions. ML is used to build predictive models, classify data, and recognize patterns, and is an essential tool for many AI applications.


-AI/ ML Lifecycle-


- The AI/ML life cycle begins with clearly defining the problem you aim to solve or the objective you want to achieve. Understanding the business context and defining success metrics are crucial at this stage.

- Whether it's optimizing a process, predicting outcomes, or automating tasks, a well-defined problem sets the direction for the entire project.

- This stage involves gathering relevant data from various sources. Data can come in different formats such as structured, unstructured, or semi-structured.

- Once collected, the data needs preprocessing, which includes cleaning, transforming, and formatting it to make it suitable for analysis. Data preprocessing ensures that the data is consistent, accurate, and ready for modeling.

-Feature engineering focuses on selecting, creating, and transforming features from the raw data to improve the performance of machine learning models. It involves identifying relevant features that contribute to the problem at hand.

- It transforming them into a format that the models can understand. Effective feature engineering can significantly impact the model's predictive power and generalization ability.

- In this stage, suitable machine learning algorithms or models are selected based on the problem type and data characteristics. The selected models are trained using the preprocessed data.

- Training involves feeding the model with input data and adjusting its parameters iteratively to minimize the difference between predicted and actual outcomes. Proper model selection and training are essential for achieving accurate and reliable predictions.

-After training, the performance of the trained models is evaluated using appropriate evaluation metrics. This stage assesses how well the models generalize to unseen data and whether they meet the predefined success criteria.

- Model evaluation helps in comparing different models, identifying strengths and weaknesses, and guiding further improvements in the AI/ML solution.

- Once a satisfactory model is obtained, it is deployed into production or the target environment where it can make predictions or assist in decision-making. Deployment involves integrating the model into existing systems or applications, ensuring scalability, reliability, and security.

- A robust deployment process is crucial for realizing the value of the AI/ML solution in real-world scenarios.

- The final stage involves continuous monitoring and maintenance of the deployed model. Monitoring helps in detecting performance degradation, concept drift, or other issues that may arise over time.

-Maintenance activities include retraining the model with new data, updating it to address changing requirements, and ensuring its continued relevance and effectiveness in solving the problem.
